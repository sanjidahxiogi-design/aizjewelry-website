Why Jewelry Prefers SUS304 Over 316L Stainless Steel

When it comes to stainless steel jewelry, two grades dominate the conversation: SUS304 and 316L. While both are valued for their durability and corrosion resistance, many fashion jewelry brands—including Shenzhen Aiz Jewelry—consistently choose SUS304 over the seemingly “higher-grade” 316L. Here’s why.

Understanding SUS304 and 316L

SUS304 Stainless Steel: Known as 18/8 stainless steel, it contains about 18% chromium and 8% nickel. It offers excellent corrosion resistance, outstanding polishability, and a smooth, lustrous finish—ideal for decorative jewelry.

316L Stainless Steel: Often called “surgical steel,” 316L contains molybdenum, which improves resistance to chlorides and harsh chemicals. It is commonly used in marine and medical applications.

On paper, 316L appears superior. But for fashion jewelry, SUS304 is often the smarter choice.

1. Cost-Effective Without Compromise

316L’s added alloys make it significantly more expensive. SUS304 delivers over 90% of the performance needed for daily wear at a much lower cost. At Shenzhen Aiz Jewelry, this allows us to invest more in design and craftsmanship—so our customers get better-looking pieces without paying for unnecessary specs.

2. Superior Surface Finish

SUS304 polishes beautifully to a high-gloss mirror or refined satin finish. It bonds exceptionally well with PVD coatings such as gold, rose gold, and black titanium. The result is jewelry that feels smooth, looks premium, and retains its beauty longer.

3. Better Workability for Creative Designs

SUS304 offers excellent ductility, making it easier to cut, stamp, engrave, and shape. This enables more intricate, lightweight, and comfortable designs. For fashion-forward collections, SUS304 gives designers greater creative freedom.

4. More Than Enough Corrosion Resistance for Daily Life

Most people don’t wear jewelry while diving in the ocean or handling industrial chemicals. SUS304 resists sweat, humidity, and everyday oxidation with ease. Under normal wearing conditions, it remains bright and rust-free for years. Only in extreme environments does 316L show a meaningful advantage.

5. Lightweight Comfort

SUS304 is slightly lighter than 316L—a small difference that becomes noticeable in earrings, stacking rings, and long necklaces, delivering all-day comfort.

When Should You Choose 316L?
316L remains the right choice for body-piercing jewelry requiring high biocompatibility, dive watches, and products specifically engineered for extreme conditions.

Final Thoughts

It allows us to create pieces that are not only visually striking but also reliable companions for everyday style.
